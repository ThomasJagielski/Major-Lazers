Mechanical System Explained

Overall System Description

  • The system starts with the user input at the keys in the front of the box. These are 3D printed with silver filament and rest on a brass rod in the center of the key. The back half of the key lever is attached to a pulley system that is connected to a rotating cylinder that sits on a copper pipe in the center of the box. This copper pipe holds the lasers and allows these rotating cylinders to move about the lasers. When the key is pressed, it rotates the cylinder so that the laser shines through the cylinder. The laser is pointed straight down to a 45 degree mirror and is then redirected to another mirror at the back of the box, which is also at a 45 degree angle. The mirror in the back redirects the laser again to straight up clear tubing, to the top where the photodiode is mounted.
  • When the user lets go of the key, there is a counterweight that causes the cylinder to block the laser in rest, and the key returns to its normal plane.
  • There is a false bottom and false sides in the enclosure design in order to hide the electrical components. The false sides allow the wires for the lasers to reach the false bottom and the false bottom covers the circuits.
  • The clear plastic pipes were used to emulate an organ, while the front was designed to emulate a keyboard.
  • The enclosure was cut using the Shopbot, the pipes were cut with the horizontal bandsaw, the acrylic (including the acrylic mirror) was laser cut, and the silver looking components were 3D printed.
  • The pulley system is made up of orange sewing thread, a steel pipe, and each end is tied to its respective 3D part.
  • The false bottom was made up of clear acrylic, then spray painted on one side with black and metallic spray paint and rastered and laser cut on the other side
  • The photodiode PCB mounts were mounted on the top of the clear pipes, attached to laser cut acrylic with slots to adjust the placement of the diode.We used M3 screws and nuts to fasten the PCB to the acrylic, as there were two holes in the PCB to do this.
